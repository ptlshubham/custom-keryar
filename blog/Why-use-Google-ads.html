Why Use Google Ads?

Google Ads can increase a company's success if the campaign is well implemented. Marketing is all about reaching out to people who want to buy your product. What better approach to contact those people than through the most popular search engine? Google Ads, together with Google Analytics and Google Trends, is an extremely effective means of Internet advertising. Businesses may reach out to anybody who uses Google to seek information, products, and services online by using Google Ads. Google Ads can deliver you a wide audience of people who are looking for the product or service you're selling. If you aren't already utilizing Google Ads, keep reading to discover more about this powerful marketing tool.

Here's how to make a good choice.

What is a Google Ad?

When you search for something on Google, have you ever seen a result labeled 'Ad'? That's just Google Ads doing what it does best. Businesses can use Google Ads to pay to get their website promoted. Websites may show in numerous Google search results depending on the keywords for which they bid, the quality score, and other factors. Recall that Google Ads and Adsense are two separate services. Whereas Adsense is used by website owners to sell ad space, Ads is used by businesses to promote their websites. And combining them may not be a good idea.

Kinds Of Google Ads

There are five different types of Google Ads, even though no two campaigns are the same:

1. Search Network campaign

With a Search Network campaign, your ad will show up not only on Google Search and Maps, but also on hundreds of other Google search partners, like YouTube and Google Shopping. When people search for terms related to a keyword for your campaign on any of these sites, they will see your ad.

2. Display Network campaign

With a Display Network campaign, you can show visual ads to people who use Google Display Network products like Gmail and YouTube.

3. Shopping campaign

With a Shopping campaign, Google will not use a user's keyword to decide how and where to show your ad on Google Shopping. Instead, it will use the product data from your web store.

4. Video campaign

A video ad for your business will be shown on YouTube and other Google Display Network sites as part of a Video campaign.

5. App campaign

With an App campaign, your ad will be shown on Google Search, YouTube, Google Play, AdMob, the Google Display Network, Google Discover, Google's search partners, and many other sites that show app ads.

Google Ads lets you choose from different ad formats to make your ads fit your marketing goals.

It lets you target

You can make your ads focus on certain keywords. You can choose who your audience is, what they like, where they live, and what language they speak. This means that you only show the ad to people who are interested and leave out everyone else. Also, Google Ads makes it easier to re-target ads to people who visited your site but didn't buy anything or do anything you wanted them to.

You can put up ads anywhere

With Google Ads, you can choose where your ad will show up. You can set the preference to only mobiles, only laptops, or a mix of mobiles, laptops, desktops, and tablets.

Tests the campaign

A/B campaign testing is a smart way to find out which filters work the best. Just let 2 or 3 campaign sets with different options be made by digital marketing experts. Choose the one that works best and get rid of the others. This is a cheap way to figure out which ad works best.

SEO takes longer to get results than PPC advertising

The time it takes to see results is the biggest difference between SEO and PPC. Almost right away, you'll see results from Google Ads. Seriously. As soon as your ad is approved, you might start getting more traffic. To get started, all you have to do is type in your carefully researched list of targeted keywords, set your maximum bid and budget, and traffic could start coming in the same day. SEO usually takes longer to show results... Occasionally a few months. SEO is important for long-term growth, but PPC is all about getting results right away.

It helps you understand your audience

You may have a good idea of the kind of people you want to come to your website. You make a campaign by choosing options that meet the requirements. But did you know that Google Ads can tell you more about the people who visit your website? From their age and gender to the devices they use and where they are, the results of your campaign tell you a lot. This lets you change your campaign for a better return on investment (ROI) the next time around.

It is transparent

In the analytics section of your Google Ads dashboard, you can see how well your ads are doing. A good digital marketing company will look at the results of the campaign to make it better for the next time. Google Ads gives information about campaigns like the cost of clicks, the keywords people use to find your website, the click-through rate, the cost per click, the number of impressions, and the number of clicks. Linking your Google Ads to Google Analytics is the best way to study how well your campaign is doing.

Budgetary flexibility

Google Ads lets you choose how much you want to spend on your campaign. You can set a limit for the whole campaign or limit it to how much you can spend each day for a certain amount of time. Yes, you can also choose when your ad will show up during the day.

Ads are only charged when clicked

When you run a campaign with Google Ads, you pay for how well it does. Even if you set a limit on how much you want to spend on ads, you will only be charged when someone clicks on one. The way you interact depends on the type of ad you chose. It could be going to your website, clicking the "install" button, or watching your video ad.
