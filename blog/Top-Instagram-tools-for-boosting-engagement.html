Top Instagram Tools for Boosting Engagement

Instagram has more than 200 million business accounts, and 1.2 billion active users are expected by 2023. The social media giant is also the fourth most popular social media site in the world. Because of things like these, Instagram is a great place to market to your audience. Get people to interact with you, and grow your business, as long as you have the right Instagram tools.

Instagram promotes products well. 72% of social media users have bought something they said. Instagram has 80%, business followers. This makes it ideal for engaging customers and advancing marketing campaigns.

This shows how much Instagram affects people. Though less popular than other social media marketing platforms, it's rising. Let me teach you how to build your Instagram audience with these fantastic tools.

However, this list is not ranked.

Free Instagram content creation tools:

Even the finest designers need assistance. These content creation multiple tools for Instagram will inspire you, and keep you updated. And also provide free, easy-to-use feed, Reels, and Stories templates.

1. Canva

Canva makes great images. Busy designers love Canvas's adaptability. The website has an Instagram feed and Stories layouts. Its drag-and-drop features let non-graphic designers create quality assets. Canva is a stock bank of photographs, animations, music, and movies for ads and reels.

Canva is free, but its subscription plan unlocks more design options including brand tool kits and the top designs.

2. Unfold

Unfold has hundreds of story templates. Squarespace's Unfold app has 200 Instagram Story templates. It has photo-editing and built-in typefaces.

Unfold is free, but its subscription plan unlocks more design templates and features.

3. Adobe Creative Cloud

Creative Cloud Express resizes and converts photos. Adobe Spark fans will love Creative Cloud Express. Adobe's drag-and-drop tool for well-designed social networking templates. Resize, background removal and file conversion make it a suitable alternative to costlier photo-editing software.

Adobe offers thousands of templates, typefaces, stock audio, and a small selection of stock photos. Adobe Creative Cloud Express is free, however, the premium plan offers additional materials, music, and design capabilities.

4. Free tools to boost interaction and gain followers:

Manage your Instagram account to boost post interaction, website traffic, and client base. Following are the IG tools that help.

5. Buffer

Buffer organizes social media content. It is a comprehensive social media management program that helps marketers. And small business owners schedule updates across numerous platforms. The tool enables you to manage social media campaigns and reply to user comments and DMs. It also offers personalized Instagram marketing advice.

6. Buffer Start Page

Start Page makes brand homepages in minutes. Buffer Start Page is a mobile landing page platform for small businesses.

For Instagram businesses without a website, Start Page optimises your link-in-bio for mobile. For businesses rebranding or changing their website, it's a terrific way to keep traffic while your main site is down.

7. Creator Studio

Creator Studio organizes digital content. Creator Studio is Meta (Instagram's parent company content)'s management solution for business accounts. It's easy to plan, manage, and monetize posts.

However, Creator Studio has several restrictions:

It's still a bit cumbersome to schedule Instagram Stories.

You can't schedule Instagram Reels.

No dynamic asset control means Creator Studio doesn't automatically reduce photos to fit the platform (unlike other tools for Instagram management and posting directly in the app).

Creator Studio is now a fantastic option for organizing in-feed, photo-based digital marketing content.

8. Free Instagram influencer and UGC tools:

Influencer marketing boosts content marketing by exposing your products and services to more people. Finding the proper influencers for your audience is difficult. These technologies locate influencers, manage labelled material, and connect you with brand-loving content makers.

9. Brand Collab Manager

Brand Collab Manager matches your business with the right influencer. Meta's Brand Collab Manager connects brands with influencers.

Instagram and Facebook members can get it free, however, there are requirements:

Follow Facebook's Branded Content Policies.

Meet Facebook's Partner Monetization Policies.

You need 1,000 followers and one of these:

15,000 post-engagements in 60 days

180,000 minutes in 60 days

30,000 one-minute views for three-minute videos in 60 days.

You must also be the page admin and submit from an eligible nation. The Brand Collab Manager helps brands find relevant influencers and creators. The tool manages influencer contracts, payments, and campaign performance.

10. ShortStack

ShortStack finds fantastic UGC. It helps businesses run Instagram contests. ShortStack's UGC management tool stands out (UGC). Instead of using the Instagram algorithm, the app will register every time a user tags you throughout the platform after you conduct a contest.

Its free plan allows endless contests and 100 entries. For additional entries and greater functionality, ShortStack offers premium plans.

11. Repost for Instagram

Repost for Instagram enables you to repost an Instagram post on your account with a few taps and credit the post owner. Before reposting photographs or videos, ask permission from the post owner and credit them in your caption. It's good for Instagram's wonderful creators and companies and is needed under Instagram's terms of service.

DM, comment, or contact the post owner to request reposting permission. You can also add a repost to Buffer using the Android or iOS mobile app.

Free Instagram analytics tools:

Refine your marketing plan with analytics. These tools show you user engagement trends so you can determine what's working and what's not.

1.Instagram Insights

For Instagram business accounts, Instagram Insights provides in-app data. It displays engagement rates, audience growth, and follower demographics.

Insights can be accessed via Instagram:

Instagram for Business account login.

Visit your profile.

Press Insights.

You can only access insights for the time you've had a business account. If you've had a personal account for five years but switched to a business account one month ago. Instagram Insights will only have data from the past month.

2. Buffer Analyze

Track critical metrics with Buffer Analyze. Buffer, a free program for planning and scheduling social media content, also has an in-depth analytics function.

Likes, comments, shares, tags, clicks, reach, and more are all displayed on a beautiful, easy-to-use dashboard. Insights are given in stunning graphics that you may screenshot for reporting. It also provides Instagram Stories analytics for free.

3. Keyhole

Keyhole monitors critical data. Analytics tool Keyhole automates reporting. Customize a dashboard to display your and your stakeholders' most essential data.

A 7-day trial of Keyhole is free. We recommend taking the time to audit your Instagram to see what's working and what may be improved.
